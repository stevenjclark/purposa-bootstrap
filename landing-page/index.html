Covid-19 and purpose

An organisation’s purpose is increasingly understood as their responsibility to people, society and the planet, above and beyond financial goals. We strongly believe the urgency and importance for organisations to act on their purpose has never been greater.

In recent months the vast majority of leaders and organisations have shown their compassion, versatility, resilience and integrity in the midst of this atrocious pandemic. Much has gone unseen but felt by well treated employees, customers or suppliers, or through support of all kinds for frontline health workers and key workers. Though without doubt we’ve also seen mistakes as well as some pretty unforgivable behaviour.

In a direct and practical way Purposa wants to help as many people as possible find and get the right job. More than ever responsible organisations want to hire people that truly get and will live their values and purpose. There are a number reasons for this, such as:

  • How we work is changing forever, with remote working there is a greater emphasis on self-awareness, motivation and doing the right thing for its own sake.
  • Organisations must radically and rapidly adapt and innovate to survive and thrive. Innovation is most effectively stimulated, steered and delivered by people engaged with purpose and values.
  • Companies must close the gap between stated purpose goals and values and what is actually being delivered. The benefits of closing this gap are essential to build trust, proven to drive growth, and of course contribute to meaningful change in the world.
